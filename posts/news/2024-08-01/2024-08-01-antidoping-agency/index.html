药检阳性归咎于食品污染即可免责？反兴奋剂机构展开调查

Antidoping Agency Acknowledges Concern Over Use of Tainted Food as Excuse

MICHAEL S. SCHMIDT, JENNY VRENTAS, TARIQ PANJA

The global antidoping regulator disclosed on Tuesday that it is investigating why athletes in China and other countries who are testing positive for banned drugs are escaping discipline through claims that they have unwittingly ingested the performance-enhancing substances through food.

世界反兴奋剂机构周二披露，它正在调查为什么中国等国运动员在违禁药物检测呈阳性反应后，声称自己是在不知情的情况下通过食物摄入了提高成绩的物质，就能得以逃避惩罚。

The statement from the World Anti-Doping Agency came after The New York Times reported earlier on Tuesday on a previously undisclosed case in which two elite Chinese swimmers who tested positive for a powerful steroid in 2022 were cleared late last year after their country’s antidoping authority blamed contaminated hamburgers.

在世界反兴奋剂机构（以下简称WADA）发表这份声明之前，《纽约时报》周二早些时候报道了一起此前未披露的案件。在该案中，两名中国顶尖水平的游泳运动员在2022年检测出强效类固醇呈阳性，在中国的反兴奋剂机构将其归咎于受污染的汉堡后，于去年底解除处罚。

It was the third incident in recent years in which China blamed food contamination for positive tests among members of its national swimming team.

这是近年来第三起中国国家游泳队队员把阳性检测结果归咎于食品污染事件。

In its statement on Tuesday, the World Anti-Doping Agency, known as WADA, revealed that at the same time it was looking into how the two swimmers had tested positive, it was also examining the previously undisclosed cases of two other Chinese athletes in different sports — shooting and BMX bike riding — who tested positive in early 2023 for trace amounts of the same banned drug, metandienone.

WADA在周二的声明中透露，在调查这两名游泳运动员阳性药检结果的同时，它还在调查另外两名其他项目的中国运动员——-射击和自由式小轮车，这也是此前未披露的案例。2023年初，这两名运动员在药检中也检测出微量的同一种违禁药物——去氢甲睾酮。

China’s antidoping regulator conducted tests that discovered metandienone in dozens of meat samples in the country, WADA said, and ultimately cleared the two other athletes as well as the two swimmers of doping.

WADA说，中国的反兴奋剂监管部门进行了检测，从数十份肉类样品中发现了去氢甲睾酮，并最终排除了这另外两名运动员和两名游泳运动员使用兴奋剂的嫌疑。

After the Chinese investigation, WADA said, it began its own inquiry early this year “to assess the circumstances, scale and risk of meat contamination with metandienone in China and other countries.”

WADA表示，在中国的调查之后，它于今年初开始了自己的调查，“以评估中国及其他国家肉类受去氢甲睾酮污染的情况、规模和风险。”

That investigation, it said, is continuing.

它表示，这项调查目前仍在继续。

“Based on the number of cases, clearly there is an issue of contamination in several countries around the world,” the agency said. “WADA is generally concerned about the number of cases that are being closed without sanction when it is not possible to challenge the contamination theory successfully” in the court system that governs sports.

“从案例的数量来看，世界上有一些国家显然存在食品受到污染问题，”该机构表示。“总的来说，让WADA感到担忧的是，”一旦在体育法庭体系内“无法对污染说做出成功的反驳，就需要在没有惩罚的情况下结案，这样的案例有很多。”

“There have been many cases of positive tests that were eventually closed without sanction as no-fault violations, sometimes with unusual methods of contamination,” the statement said.

声明称：“已经有很多检测呈阳性的案例，最终作为无过错违规行为而不予处罚结案，有时污染的途径很不寻常。”

WADA said the problem extended beyond China to countries including the United States, where, it said, there have been several cases in recent months involving “highly intricate contamination scenarios” that were accepted as legitimate reason not to punish athletes. It did not provide any details.

WADA表示，这一问题不仅限于中国，美国等国家也存在，近期也出现了涉及“高度复杂污染场景”的几起案例，作为不惩罚运动员的正当理由。该机构没有提供任何细节。

The admission from WADA — which is supposed to serve as a backstop when countries fail to properly police their athletes — was a change in its tone and posture after months in which it has denied that positive tests were being mishandled.

从WADA的此番承认可以看到，它在语气和姿态上都做出了转变，此前几个月，该机构一直否认自己对阳性兴奋剂检测结果处理不当。该机构本应在各国未能对运动员进行适当监管时提供支持。

The disclosures came at a time when WADA and the entire global antidoping system are under intensifying scrutiny. The Times reported in April that 23 Chinese swimmers who tested positive for a different performance-enhancing drug months before the Tokyo Olympics in 2021 had also been cleared of doping after China’s antidoping regulator concluded that they had unwittingly consumed the substance through food at a hotel where they were staying.

这些披露正值WADA和全球反兴奋剂系统受到越来越严厉的审视之际。据《纽约时报》4月报道，在2021年东京奥运会前几个月，23名中国游泳运动员在另一种提高成绩的药物检测中呈阳性，在中国的反兴奋剂部门认定他们是在不知情的情况下，在下榻酒店无意摄入含有这种药物的食物后，他们使用兴奋剂的嫌疑也被排除。

On Capitol Hill, a bipartisan group from the House and the Senate ramped up its pressure on the agency, holding a news conference on Tuesday to unveil legislation that would give the United States more leverage over WADA by allowing the White House to withhold funding from the agency. The United States, through a congressional appropriation, contributes more money each year — expected to be about $3.6 million in 2024 — to the agency’s roughly $50 million annual budget than any other country.

在国会，参众两院的一个两党小组加大了对该机构的施压力度，周二它举行了一场新闻发布会，公布了一项立法，允许白宫停止对该机构的资金支持，从而使美国对WADA拥有更大的影响力。美国每年通过国会拨款，为该机构提供的资金比其他国家都要多，预计2024年将达到约360万美元。WADA的年度预算约为5000万美元。

“Our message is simple: We will not be silenced for trying to promote fair play,” said Senator Marsha Blackburn, Republican of Tennessee, who is one of the bill’s sponsors.

该法案的发起人之一、田纳西州共和党参议员玛莎·布莱克本说，“我们要传达的信息很简单：为了努力推动公平竞争，我们是不会沉默的。”

She added that there was deep unease in Washington over a recent effort by the International Olympic Committee and WADA to press the United States to stop a federal investigation into how the positive tests of the 23 swimmers in the previous case were handled. The I.O.C. last week awarded the 2034 Winter Olympics to Salt Lake City only on the condition that the United States not undermine WADA’s authority over doping cases.

她还说，国际奥委会和WADA最近向美国施压，要求停止对前述23名中国游泳运动员检测结果呈阳性的处理方式展开联邦调查，华盛顿对此深感不安。国际奥委会上周将2034年冬奥会的举办权授予盐湖城，但条件是美国不得削弱WADA在兴奋剂案件上的权威。

Ms. Blackburn was joined on the call by Representative John Moolenaar, the Michigan Republican who is the chairman of the House select committee on China. The two other lawmakers behind the legislation were Democrats: Senator Chris Van Hollen of Maryland, who is the chairman of a Senate appropriations subcommittee, and Representative Raja Krishnamoorthi of Illinois, who serves on the House China committee.

与布莱克本一起通话的还有众议院中国问题特设委员会主席、密歇根州共和党众议员约翰·穆勒纳尔。一起提出该法案的另外两位议员是民主党人，分别是马里兰州参议员克里斯·范霍伦和伊利诺伊州众议员拉贾·克里希纳莫尔蒂，前者是参议院拨款小组委员会主席，后者是众议院中国委员会成员。

The controversy around China has led to scrutiny over the entire global antidoping system, and in particular how much faith is placed in national bodies to police their own athletes.

围绕中国的争议引发了对全球反兴奋剂体系的审视，尤其是对国家机构监督本国运动员的信任程度。

Just before the Olympics, questions were raised over how British officials were able to clear Jade Jones, a double Olympic taekwondo champion, to try to secure a third title in Paris after she refused to provide a doping sample to drug testers. Such a refusal would typically result in a ban. She was cleared because of “exceptional circumstances” that have never been fully revealed.

就在本届奥运会之前，有人质疑英国官员为何允许两届奥运会跆拳道冠军杰德·琼斯在拒绝向药检人员提供兴奋剂样本后获得参赛资格，并试图在巴黎争夺第三个冠军的。这样的不合作通常会导致禁赛。她之所以免于惩罚，是因为至今尚未完全披露出来的“特殊情况”。

Katie Ledecky, an 11-time Olympic medalist, said that before swimming the preliminaries of the 1,500 freestyle on Tuesday morning, she had read the Times article about the two Chinese swimmers being cleared on the basis of food contamination.

11枚奥运奖牌得主凯蒂·莱德基说，在周二上午参加1500米自由泳预赛之前，她已经读了时报关于两名中国游泳运动员基于食物污染的理由而免于惩罚的报道。

“I think I’ve made my thoughts pretty clear,” said Ledecky, who has previously expressed disappointment about a lack of transparency by antidoping authorities and the potential that clean swimmers might be competing against rivals who use banned substances. “It’s disappointing.”

“我想我已经把我的想法说得很清楚了，”莱德基说。此前，她曾对反兴奋剂机构缺乏透明度，以及清白的游泳运动员可能与使用违禁药物的对手竞争表示失望。“这令人失望。”

Ledecky could end up swimming against as many as two Chinese athletes who tested positive in recent years when she competes in the 4x200 freestyle relay on Thursday.

莱德基可能会在周四参加4×200米自由泳接力赛时，与近几年检测结果呈阳性的两名中国运动员同场竞技。

Team U.S.A.’s athlete’s commission released a statement saying it was “extremely angered and discouraged” by the latest report of positive tests among Chinese athletes and the decisions by WADA and swimming’s global organization not to take action.

美国队的运动员委员会发表声明说，对于中国运动员检测呈阳性的最新报道，以及WADA和游泳项目国际组织决定不采取行动的做法，该委员会感到“极其愤怒和沮丧”。

“As our athletes currently compete on the largest global stage in Paris, they deserve the opportunity to do so with the knowledge that the competition is fair and in accordance with the rules,” the statement said.

声明说：“我们的运动员目前正在巴黎的全球最大舞台上竞技，他们有权在公平并符合规则的氛围中竞争。”

Three of the Chinese swimmers competing at the Paris Games who tested positive in 2021 for a banned substance — a heart medication — but were cleared on the basis of food contamination said in interviews with The Times that they had not doped and pointed to their being cleared by antidoping regulators.

参加巴黎奥运会的三名中国游泳运动员曾在2021年因一种违禁物质（一种心脏药物）检测呈阳性，但以食品污染为由而未受处罚。他们在接受时报采访时表示，自己没有服用兴奋剂，并指出，反兴奋剂机构已经证明了他们的清白。

“We are clean,” said Yang Junxuan, one of three swimmers who The Times reported also tested positive for a different banned substance several years earlier. “The results we are having now are the results of our scientific method of training.”

“我们是干净的，”杨浚瑄说道，她是《纽约时报》报道中提到的几年前检出其他禁药的三名游泳运动员之一。“我们现在的成绩是我们科学训练的结果。”
