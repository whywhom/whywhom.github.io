在传统的眼光里，如果一位“贤妻良母”意外收获了一笔小财富，她多半会为自己的孩子或者为整个家，置办点新东西。但凯特·肖邦笔下的萨默斯太太并未如此。
萨默斯太太用那一小笔意外之财，吃了丰盛可口的午餐，买了柔顺丝滑的丝袜，踩着丝袜去享受了一场美轮美奂的视听盛宴。
这些其实都是她少女时期的生活方式，但结婚后的她被迫舍弃了所有的生活享受，被迫成为一个社会所期望的“贤妻良母”，生活在无边的压抑，空虚，庸常，无趣里。

A Pair of Silk Stockings

一双长丝袜

作者 凯特·肖邦
译者 齐彦婧
出版 浙江教育出版社

Little Mrs. Sommers one day found herself the unexpected possessor of fifteen dollars. It seemed to her a very large amount of money, and the way in which it stuffed and bulged her worn old porte-monnaie gave her a feeling of importance such as she had not enjoyed for years.

一天，小个子妇人萨默斯太太意外翻到十五美元。这可不是个小数目，她望着那只鼓鼓囊囊的旧钱包，感觉很久没这么开心了。

  • possessor [pəˈzesər] n. The possessor of something is the person who has it. 拥有者; 所有者; 持有者
  • stuff [stʌf] v. If you stuff a container or space with something, you fill it with something or with a quantity of things until it is full. 把…装满
  • bulge [bʌldʒ] v. If you say that something is bulging with things, you are emphasizing that it is full of them. 塞满
  • porte-monnaie n. 钱包

The question of investment was one that occupied her greatly. For a day or two she walked about apparently in a dreamy state, but really absorbed in speculation andcalculation. She did not wish to act hastily, to do anything she might afterward regret. But it was during the still hours of the night when she lay awake revolving plans in her mind that she seemed to see her way clearly toward a proper and judicious use of the money.

这钱怎么花？她左思右想，有一两天都是恍恍惚惚的，一直在心里拿捏盘算。她不想操之过急，免得将来后悔。但夜深人静时，她睁着眼睛躺在床上继续琢磨，渐渐厘清了思路，为这笔钱找到了最合适、最明智的用途。

  • dreamy [ˈdriːmi] adj. If you describe something as dreamy, you mean that you like it and that it seems gentle and soft, like something in a dream. 如梦般轻柔的
  • speculation [ˌspekjuˈleɪʃn] n. the act of forming opinions about what has happened or what might happen without knowing all the facts 推测，猜测
  • calculation [ˌkælkjuˈleɪʃn] n. the process of working something out mathematically 计算
  • hastily [ˈheɪstɪli] adv. in a hurried or hasty manner 匆忙地，急速地
  • judicious [dʒuˈdɪʃəs] adj. If you describe an action or decision as judicious, you approve of it because you think that it shows good judgment and sense. 明智的

A dollar or two should be added to the price usually paid for Janie’s shoes, which would insure their lasting an appreciable time longer than they usually did. She would buy so and so many yards of percale for new shirt waists for the boys and Janie and Mag. She had intended to make the old ones doby skillful patching. Mag should have another gown. She had seen some beautiful patterns, veritable bargains in the shop windows. And still there would be left enough for new stockings–two pairs apiece–and what darning that would save for a while! She would get caps for the boys and sailor-hats for the girls. The vision of her little brood looking fresh and dainty and new for once in theirlives excited her and made her restless and wakeful with anticipation.

给贾妮买鞋时她要多花一两块钱，贵点的鞋子能多穿很久。她还要多买几码高级密织棉，用来给儿子们还有贾妮和玛格做新衬衫，她之前本想给旧衬衫打几个巧妙的补丁，让他们将就穿的。玛格该添置一件新礼服了。她在商店橱窗里看见一个漂亮的款式，价格也很公道。剩下的钱还能再买几双长筒袜——一人两双——这样她可以少做多少针线活儿啊！她要给儿子们买便帽，给女儿们买水手帽。想到孩子们终于可以打扮一新，穿一回漂亮衣服，她满怀期待，躺在床上兴奋地翻来覆去，难以成眠。

  • appreciable [əˈpriːʃəbl] adj. large enough to be important or clearly noticed (数量或效果)可观的
  • percale [pərˈkeɪl] n. a type of cotton fabric, used especially to make bed linen 棉布
  • patching [ˈpætʃɪŋ] n. a piece of material that you use to cover a hole in something 补丁
  • gown [ɡaʊn] n. adress, usually a long dress, which women wear on formal occasions (女用) 长礼服
  • veritable [ˈverɪtəbl] adj. You can use veritable to emphasize the size, amount, or nature of something. 名副其实的
  • bargain [ˈbɑːrɡən] n. Somethingthat is a bargain is good value, usually because it has been sold at a lower price than normal. 物美价廉的商品
  • apiece [əˈpiːs] adv. If people have a particular number of things apiece, they have that number each. 每人地
  • darn [dɑːrn] v. If you darn something knitted or made of cloth, you repair ahole in it by sewing stitches across the hole and then weaving stitches in and out of them. 织补
  • brood [bruːd] n. You can refer to someone’s young children as their brood when you want to emphasize that there are a lot of them. 一家子
  • dainty [ˈdeɪnti] adj. small, delicate, and pretty 娇小漂亮的
  • anticipation [ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃn] n. a feeling of excitement about something pleasant or exciting that you know is going to happen 期待

The neighbors sometimes talked of certain “better days"that little Mrs. Sommers had known before she had ever thought of being Mrs. Sommers. She herself indulged in no such morbid retrospection. She had no time–no second of time to devote to the past. The needs of the present absorbed her every faculty. A vision of the future like some dim, gaunt monster sometimes appalled her, but luckily tomorrow never comes.

邻居们有时会提起萨默斯太太出嫁前过的那种“好日子”，但她自己从不无谓地沉湎于过去。她没有时间——一秒钟都没有。如今，生活耗尽了她所有的精力。有时，她会觉得未来就像一只怪兽，黯淡、憔悴、面目可憎，万幸的是，她没有未来。

  • indulge in 沉湎于，沉溺于
  • morbid [ˈmɔːrbɪd] adj. interested in unpleasant things, especially death 病态的
  • retrospection [ˌretrəˈspekʃn] n. the act of recalling things past, especially in one’s personal experience 回顾
  • faculty [ˈfæklti] n. physical and mental abilities 机能
  • dim [dɪm] adj. not bright 昏暗的
  • gaunt [ɡɔːnt] adj. If someone looks gaunt, they look very thin, usually because they have been very ill or worried. 憔悴的

Mrs. Sommers was one who knew the value of bargains; who could stand for hours making her way inch by inch toward the desired object that wasselling below cost. She could elbow her way if need be; she had learned to clutch a piece of goods and hold it and stick to it with persistence and determination till her turn came to be served, no matter when it came.

萨默斯太太最喜欢买折扣商品，她能一连排好几个小时的队，一点一点往前挪，只因为想要的东西正在打折。必要时她能左推右挤给自己开出一条路，还会毅然决然地抓住一样东西久久不放，只要能得到它，等多久都不在乎。

  • elbow [ˈelboʊ] v. If you elbow people aside or elbow your way somewhere, you push people with your elbows in order to move somewhere. (用肘部) 推
  • clutch [klʌtʃ] v. If you clutch at something or clutch something,you hold it tightly, usually because you are afraid or anxious. (因为害怕或焦虑而) 抓牢
  • persistence [pərˈsɪstəns] n. If you have persistence, you continueto do something even though it is difficult or other people are against it. 坚持不懈; 执著

But that day she was a little faint and tired. She had swallowed alight luncheon–no! when she came to think of it, between getting the children fed and the place righted, and preparing herself for the shopping bout, she had actually forgotten to eat any luncheon at all!

不过那天，她有点头晕，又有点疲乏，中午只匆匆地扒了几口吃的——不对！她仔细一想，自己光顾着喂孩子们吃饭和收拾屋子了，接着又一门心思想着出门采购的事，最后竟连午饭都忘吃了！

  • faint [feɪnt] adj. feeling weak and tired and likely to become unconscious 昏眩的，快要昏厥的
  • luncheon [ˈlʌntʃən] n. a formal lunch, for example, to celebrate an important event or to raise money for charity 午宴
  • bout [baʊt] n. a short period of great activity 一阵，一场

She sat herself upon a revolving stool before a counter that was comparatively deserted, trying to gather strength and courage to charge through an eager multitude that was besieging breastworks of shirting and figured lawn. An all-gone limp feeling had come over her and she rested her hand aimlessly upon the counter. She wore no gloves. By degrees she grew aware that her handhad encountered something very soothing, very pleasant to touch. She looked down to see that her hand lay upon a pile of silk stockings. A placard nearby announced that they had been reduced in price from two dollars and fifty cents to one dollar and ninety-eight cents; and a young girl who stood behind the counter asked her if she wished to examine their line of silk hosiery. She smiled, just as if she had been asked to inspect a tiara of diamonds with the ultimate view of purchasing it. But she went on feeling the soft, sheeny luxurious things–with both hands now, holding them up to see them glisten, and to feel them glide serpent-like through her fingers.

激动的人群把卖衬衫和印花细麻布的柜台围了个水泄不通，她找到一个相对冷清的柜台，坐到转椅上，深吸一口气，准备杀进重围。但她突然泄了气，整个人瘫软下来，随意把手往柜台上一搁。她没戴手套，忽然感觉碰到了什么手感绝佳的东西，摸着舒服极了。她低下头，看见自己的手正搁在一叠长丝袜上。旁边的标签显示丝袜正在打折，原价两块五，现价一块九毛八。这时，柜台里那位年轻的女售货员问她想不想看看这些丝袜。她笑了，感觉那女孩就像在问自己付款前要不要再仔细瞧瞧这顶钻石王冠。她继续用两只手抚摸那些柔软光亮的高级织物，捧起它们，仔细端详上面细密的闪光，任它们像蛇一样滑过她指尖。

  • stool [stuːl] n. a seat with legs but no support for your arms or back 凳子
  • multitude [ˈmʌltɪtuːd] n. a very large number of people 一大群人

  • breastwork [‘brest,wɜːrk] n. temporary defensive work, usually breast-high 临时胸墙
  • lawn [lɔːn] n. a fine linen or cotton fabric, used for clothing (衣服用)细麻

  • soothing [ˈsuːðɪŋ] adj. 使人宽心的
  • placard [ˈplækɑːrd] n. a large notice that is carried in a march or displayed in a public place 标语牌; 布告
  • hosiery [ˈhoʊʒəri] n. stockings, socks, and tights, especially when they are on sale in stores (尤指商店里出售的)袜类
  • tiara [tiˈɑːrə] n. a metal band shaped like half a circle and decorated with jewels that girls or women wear on their heads (女式)冕状头饰
  • sheeny [‘ʃi:ni] adj. reflecting light 有光泽的
  • luxurious [lʌɡˈʒʊriəs] adj. comfortable and expensive 豪华的
  • glisten [ˈɡlɪsn] v. If something glistens, it shines, usually because it is wet or oily. (因有水或有油而) 发光 v.
  • glide [ɡlaɪd] v. move silently and in a smooth and effortless way 滑行
  • serpent [ˈsɜːrpənt] n. a snake 蛇

Two hectic blotches came suddenly into her pale cheeks. She looked up at the girl. “Do you think there are any eights-and-a-half among these?”

萨默斯太太苍白的双颊泛起红晕。她抬头望着那女孩。
“有八号半的吗？”

  • hectic [ˈhektɪk] adj. very busy and involves a lot of rushedactivity 紧张忙碌的
  • blotch [blɑːtʃ] n. a small unpleasant-looking area of colour, for example, on someone’s skin 色斑

There were any number of eights-and-a-half. In fact, there were more of that size than any other. Here was a light-blue pair; there were some lavender, some all black and various shades of tan and gray. Mrs. Sommers selected a black pair and looked at them very long and closely. She pretended to be examining their texture, which the clerk assured her was excellent.

他们有的是八号半的丝袜。实际上，柜台里就数八号半的最多，有一双浅蓝的，一双淡紫的，还有几双纯黑的，外加深浅不一的棕色和灰色。萨默斯太太挑出一双黑色的，拿在手里久久端详，假装查看它的质地，不过售货员向她保证，这袜子的质地是最好的。

  • lavender [ˈlævəndər] adj. 薰衣草紫的
  • shade [ʃeɪd] n. A shade of a particular colour is one of its different forms. 色调
  • tan [tæn] n. 棕褐色
  • texture [ˈtekstʃər] n. The texture of something is the way that it feels when you touch it, for example, how smooth or rough it is. 手感
  • clerk [klɜːrk] n. Someone who sells things to customers in a shop. 售货员

“A dollar and ninety-eight cents,” she mused aloud.“Well, I’ll take this pair.” She handed the girl a five-dollar bill and waited for her change and for her parcel. What a very small parcel it was! It seemed lost in the depths of her shabby old shopping-bag.

“一块九毛八。”她喃喃自语，“这双我要了。”她递给女孩一张五美元的钞票，等她找钱、打包。这包裹多小啊！放进她那只寒酸的旧购物袋一下子就找不着了。

  • muse [mjuːz] v. If you muse on something, you think about it, usually saying or writing what you are thinking at the same time. 揣摩
  • parcel [ˈpɑːrsl] n. something wrapped in paper, in a bag or large envelope, or in a box, usually so that it can be sent to someone by mail 包裹; 邮包

Mrs. Sommers after that did not move in the direction of the bargain counter. She took the elevator, which carried her to an upper floor into the region of the ladies’ waiting-rooms. Here, in a retired corner, she exchanged her cotton stockings for the new silk ones which she had just bought. She was not going through any acute mental process or reasoning with herself, nor was she striving to explain to her satisfaction the motive of her action. She was not thinking at all. She seemed for the time to be taking a rest from that laborious and fatiguing function and to have abandoned herself to some mechanical impulse that directed her actions and freed her of responsibility.

买完丝袜，萨默斯太太并没冲向折扣柜台，而是乘电梯上楼，来到女士休息室。她躲在角落脱下棉袜，换上新买的丝袜，根本没过脑子，也没经过什么尖锐的思想斗争，更没刻意去想自己为什么这么做。她其实什么也没想。当时，她大概是给自己的脑袋放了假，放弃了艰巨而沉重的思考，彻底把自己交给本能的冲动，让它主导自己的行为，解除自己的责任。

  • acute [əˈkjuːt] adj. If a person’s or animal’s sight, hearing, or sense of smell is acute, it is sensitive and powerful. 敏锐的，尖锐的
  • strive [straɪv] v. to make a great effort to do something or get it 努力
  • laborious [ləˈbɔːriəs] adj. takes a lot of time and effort 费时费力的
  • fatiguing [fəˈtiːɡɪŋ] adj. makes you feel extremely physically or mentally tired 使人疲惫的。
  • mechanical [məˈkænɪkl] adj. If you describe someone’s action as mechanical, you mean that they do it automatically, without thinking about it. 机械似的
  • impulse [ˈɪmpʌls] n. a sudden desire to do something 冲动

How good was the touch of the raw silk to her flesh! She felt like lying back in the cushioned chair and revelling for a while in the luxury of it. She did for a little while. Then she replaced her shoes, rolled the cotton stockings together and thrust them into her bag. After doing this she crossed straight over to the shoe department and took her seat to be fitted.

天然的丝绸紧贴着皮肤，那感觉是多么美妙！她真想靠在带衬垫的椅背上尽情享受这片刻的奢侈。她靠了一小会儿，然后穿上鞋，卷起棉袜塞进包里，直奔女鞋部，坐下来试鞋。

  • raw [rɔː] adj. Raw materials or substances are in their natural state before being processed or used in manufacturing. 未加工的，天然的
  • cushion [ˈkʊʃn] n. a fabric case filled with soft material, which you put on a seat to make it more comfortable 坐垫
  • revel [ˈrevl] v. to enjoy something very much 陶醉; 沉湎
  • thrust [θrʌst] v. push or move something somethere quickly with a lot of force 猛推; 猛塞; 猛刺

She was fastidious. The clerk could not make her out; he could not reconcile her shoes with her stockings, and she was not too easily pleased. She held back her skirts and turned her feet one way and her head another way as she glanced down at the polished, pointed-tipped boots. Her foot and ankle looked very pretty. She could not realize that they belonged to her and were apart of herself. She wanted an excellent and stylish fit, she told the young fellow who served her, and she did not mind the difference of a dollar or two more in the price so long as she got what she desired.

她眼光很高，售货员猜不透她的喜好，找来的鞋总不配她的丝袜。而且她也很难伺候。她提着裙子，盯着脚上那双锃亮的尖头靴子，头摆来摆去，脚尖变换着方向。她的脚背和脚踝好看极了，好看到不像她自己的。她告诉那位男售货员，她想买一双合脚又漂亮的鞋子，只要她满意，贵一两块无所谓。

  • fastidious [fæˈstɪdiəs] adj. If you say that someone is fastidious, you mean that they pay great attention to detail because they like everything to be very neat, accurate, and in good order. 极其注重细节的
  • reconcile [ˈrekənsaɪl] v. If you reconcile two beliefs, facts, or demands that seem to be opposed or completely different, you find a way in which they can both be true or both be successful. 使和谐一致; 调和
  • boot [buːt] n. shoes that cover your whole foot and the lower part of your leg 靴子

It was a long time since Mrs. Sommers had been fitted with gloves. On rare occasions when she had bought a pair they were always “bargains,” so cheap that it would have been preposterous and unreasonable to have expected them to be fitted to the hand.

萨默斯太太已经很久没买过手套了，偶尔买一双也都是“打折货”，便宜得根本不可能、也没理由合适。

  • preposterous [prɪˈpɑːstərəs] adj. extremely unreasonable and foolish. 荒谬的

Now she rested her elbow on the cushion of the glove counter, and a pretty, pleasant young creature, delicate and deft of touch, drew along-wristed “kid” over Mrs. Sommers’s hand. She smoothed it down over the wrist and buttoned it neatly, and both lost themselves for a second or two in admiring contemplation of the little symmetrical gloved hand. But therewere other places where money might be spent.

这会儿，她把胳膊肘枕在手套柜台的软垫上，让一位美丽可人、动作灵巧的年轻女孩给她套上一只羔羊皮长手套。女孩顺着手腕理好手套，一丝不苟地扣上扣子，两个人目不转睛地欣赏手套里那只匀称的小手，看得如痴如醉。不过她可没工夫耽搁，还得去别处花钱呢。

  • deft [deft] adj. skillful and often quick 灵巧的
  • contemplation [ˌkɑːntəmˈpleɪʃn] n. a calm lengthy intent consideration 沉思
  • symmetrical [sɪˈmetrɪkl] adj. If something is symmetrical, it has two halves which are exactly the same, except that one half is the mirror image of the other. 对称的

There were books and magazines piled up in the window of a stall a few paces down the street. Mrs. Sommers bought two high-priced magazines such as she had been accustomed to read in the days when she had been accustomed to other pleasant things. She carried them without wrapping. As well as she could she lifted her skirts at the crossings. Her stockings and boots and well fitting gloves had worked marvels in her bearing–had given her a feeling of assurance, a sense of belonging to the well-dressed multitude.

街上不远处有个报刊亭，橱窗里总是摆满一摞摞书报杂志。萨默斯太太买了两本昂贵的杂志，都是她过去生活优渥时爱读的。她没让店家包起来，而是直接拿在手上。每经过一个十字路口，她都会把裙子高高提起。穿上漂亮的袜子、靴子，戴上合手的手套，她顿时变得气度不凡——整个人仿佛有了底气，好像自己也是那种光鲜靓丽的女人。

  • stall [stɔːl] n. a large table on which you put goods that you want to sell, or information that you want to give people 货摊; 咨询台
  • be accustomed to 习惯于
  • marvel [ˈmɑːrvl] n. You can describe something or someone as a marvel to indicate that you think that they are wonderful. 奇迹

She was very hungry. Another time she would have stilled the cravings for food until reaching her own home, where she would have brewed herself a cup of tea and taken a snack of anything that was available. But the impulse that was guiding her would not suffer her to entertain any such thought.

她饿极了。要在平时，她一定会咽着口水匆匆回家给自己泡杯茶，再吃点零食什么的。但那股冲动指引着她，不允许她有这种想法。

  • still v. to become calm and quiet; to make sth calm and quiet（使）静止，平静，安静：
  • craving [ˈkreɪvɪŋ] n. a strong desire for something 强烈的愿望，渴望
  • brew [bruː] v. If you brew tea or coffee, you make it by pouring hot water over tea leaves or ground coffee. 冲泡 (茶、咖啡等)

There was a restaurant at the corner. She had never entered its doors; from the outside she had sometimes caught glimpses of spotless damask and shining crystal, and soft-stepping waiters serving people of fashion.

街角就有一家餐馆。她从没进去过，只在偶尔路过门外时瞥见过里面完美无瑕的花缎餐巾、晶莹剔透的水晶杯盏，还有侍者迈着轻盈的脚步为上流人士服务。

  • catch glimpses of sth 瞥见
  • spotless [ˈspɑːtləs] adj. completely clean 没有污点的; 一尘不染的
  • damask [ˈdæməsk] n. a type of heavy cloth with a pattern woven intoit 织锦
  • crystal [ˈkrɪstl] n. a transparent rock that is used to make jewellery and or naments 水晶

When she entered her appearance created no surprise, no consternation, as she had half feared it might. She seated herself at a small table alone, and an attentive waiter at once approached to take her order. She did not want a profusion; she craved a nice and tasty bite–a half dozen blue-points, a plump chop with cress, a something sweet–a creme-frappee, for instance; a glass of Rhine wine, and after all a small cup of black coffee.

她起初还怕引人侧目，但她走进餐厅却没有引起任何震动。她在一张小桌前就座，一位殷勤的侍者立刻过来替她点单。她不想吃正餐，只想来点可口的小食——半打蓝点牡蛎，一份猪扒配水芹，至于甜点——就奶昔吧，外加一杯莱因红酒，餐后再来一小杯黑咖啡。

  • consternation [ˌkɑːnstərˈneɪʃn] n. a feeling of anxiety or fear 恐慌
  • attentive [əˈtentɪv] adj. helpful and polite 体贴的; 殷勤的
  • profusion [prəˈfjuːʒn] n. a very large quantity or variety of it 大量; 丰富
  • plump [plʌmp] adj. fat or rounded 丰满的
  • chop [tʃɑːp] n. a small piece of meat cut from the ribs of a sheep or pig 排骨
  • cress [kres] n. a plant with small green leaves that are used in salads or to decorate food水芹
  • crème-frappe [ kriːm fræˈpeɪ] n. 奶昔

While waiting to be served she removed her gloves very leisurely and laid them beside her. Then she picked up a magazine and glanced through it, cutting the pages with a blunt edge of her knife. It was all very agreeable. The damask was even more spotless than it had seemed through the window, andthe crystal more sparkling. There were quiet ladies and gentlemen, who did notnotice her, lunching at the small tables like her own. A soft, pleasing strain of music could be heard, and a gentle breeze, was blowing through the window. She tasted a bite, and she read a word or two, and she sipped the amber wine and wiggled her toes in the silk stockings. The price of it made no difference. She counted the money out to the waiter and left an extra coin on his tray, whereupon he bowed before her as before a princess of royal blood.

等餐时，她不慌不忙地摘下手套放在一旁，然后拿起一本杂志翻看，用刀背裁开书页。一切都是那么惬意。花缎餐巾比她在门外看到的还要洁白，水晶杯也更加晶莹。附近的小桌上坐的全是绅士淑女，所有人都在安静地用餐，根本没人盯着她看。她听着柔美动听的音乐，感受着窗外吹来的微风，吃几口东西，读几个字，呷一口琥珀色的美酒，在丝袜里舒展脚趾。令人咋舌的账单并没影响她的心情。她把钱付给侍者，又在他的托盘上多放了一枚硬币，他鞠躬道谢，仿佛她是位出身高贵的公主。

  • blunt [blʌnt] adj. A blunt knife or blade is no longer sharp and does not cut well. 不锋利的
  • strain [streɪn] n. a particular type 类型; 品种
  • breeze [briːz] n. a gentle wind 微风
  • sip [sɪp] v. drink by taking just a small amount at a time 小口地喝
  • amber [ˈæmbər] adj. 琥珀色的
  • wiggle [ˈwɪɡl] v. If you wiggle something or if it wiggles, it moves up and down or from side to side in small quick movements. 摆动; 扭动

There was still money in her purse, and her next temptation presented itself in the shape of a matinee poster.

她的钱还没花完。接下来，她被一张剧场海报吸引，想好了下一个去处。

  • matinee [ˌmætnˈeɪ] n. A matinee is a performance of a play or a showing of a film which takes place in the afternoon. (戏剧、电影的) 午后场

It was a little later when she entered the theatre, the play had begun and the house seemed to her to be packed. But there were vacant seats here and there, and into one of them she was ushered, between brilliantly dressed women who had gone there to kill time and eat candy and display their gaudy attire. There were many others who were there solely for the play and acting. It is safe to say there was no one present who bore quite the attitude which Mrs. Sommers did to her surroundings. She gathered in the whole–stage and players and people in one wide impression, and absorbed it and enjoyed it.She laughed at the comedy and wept–she and the gaudy woman next to her wept over the tragedy. And they talked a little together over it. And the gaudy woman wiped her eyes and sniffled on a tiny square of filmy, perfumed lace and passed little Mrs. Sommers her box of candy.

她走进剧场时，戏已经开场了，乍一看，台下几乎座无虚席，不过依然有零星的空位散落各处。她被领到一个空位上，身边全是穿着入时的妇人，都是来这里打发时间的，顺便吃点糖果、显摆显摆自己华贵的服饰。也有不少人是专门冲着戏和演员来的。不过可以说，这里没有一个人像萨默斯太太那么在意周围的环境。她把剧场里的一切——舞台、演员还有观众——统统看在眼里，记在心里，细细品味。看到滑稽的地方她就哈哈大笑，进入悲伤的剧情她就跟旁边那位时髦的妇人一起痛哭流涕。她们还交换了几句意见。那位妇人拿出一方香气四溢的蕾丝薄手帕，用它揩眼泪、擤鼻涕，又递给萨默斯太太一些糖果。

  • usher [ˈʌʃər] v. If you usher someone somewhere, you show them where they should go by going with them. 引领
  • gaudy [ˈɡɔːdi] adj. very brightly coloured and showy 花哨的
  • attire [əˈtaɪər] n. the clothes one is wearing 服装
  • solely [ˈsoʊlli] adv. If something involves solely one thing, it involves only this thing and no others. 惟一地
  • weep over 为…流泪
  • sniffle [ˈsnɪfl] v. If you sniffle, you keep sniffing, usually because you are crying or have a cold. 抽噎
  • lace [leɪs] n. Lace is a very delicate cloth which is made with a lot of holes in it. It is made by twisting together very fine threads of cotton to form decorative patterns. 花边

The play was over, the music ceased, the crowd filed out. It was like a dream ended. People scattered in all directions. Mrs. Sommers went to the corner and waited for the cable car.

戏剧落幕，音乐停止，观众纷纷离场，仿佛做完了一场美梦。人群涌向各个方向。萨默斯太太去街角等她的电车。

  • cease [siːs] v. stops happening or existing 停止
  • scatter [ˈskætər] v. If a group of people scatter or if you scatter them, they suddenly separate and move in different directions.散开

A man with keen eyes, who sat opposite to her, seemed to like the study of her small, pale face. It puzzled him to decipher what he saw there. In truth, he saw nothing-unless he were wizard enough to detect a poignant wish, a powerful longing that the cable car would never stop anywhere, but go on and on with her forever.

电车上有个眼尖的男人坐在她对面，饶有兴味地观察她那张苍白的小脸，却看不懂她的神情。其实他什么也没看见——除非他会魔法，能读出那个忧伤的心愿：她多希望这趟电车永不会停，能载着她一直向前，直到永远。

  • decipher [dɪˈsaɪfər] v.to succeed in finding the meaning of sth that is difficult to read or understand 破译，辨认（难认、难解的东西）
  • wizard [ˈwɪzərd] n. a man who has magic powers 男巫
  • detect [dɪˈtekt] v.to discover or notice sth, especially sth that is not easy to see, hear, etc. 发现；查明；侦察出
  • poignant [ˈpɔɪnjənt] adj. Something that is poignant affects you deeply and makes you feel sadness or regret. 辛酸的
