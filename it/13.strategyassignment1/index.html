Abstract

Big data as a concept is having increasing relevance in a data driven world. Big data analytics serves as a tool to process varied dimensions of data in a big data environment. The application instances of data analytics across sectors brings about numerous advantages as cited in this document. The organizations that implement Big data possess characteristics that are data driven and information centric with a centralized data storage, along with other features elaborated in the document. This report also describes the professional role of a CIO in a big data organization and the ethical, security and governance challenges he generally face in managing such an enterprise. The report after thoroughly discussing all these tenets conclude by inspecting the strategic plan of NASA where big data is implemented in its entirety.  

Introduction

In the current data driven world, the data is getting generated at a rapid speed and the amount of data is huge, these data considered as assets that creates value (Avinash & Babu, 2018). To discover the meaningful value out of those data, there needs to have proper built in architectures and technologies. The emergence of Big data analytics fits in to cover these needs. Big data analytics is the process that is used to examine various data sets to discover hidden patterns, correlations among data, analyze market trends and other useful information that help organizations to make important decisions (Chauhan, Agarwal, & Kar, 2016; Henry & Venkatraman, 2015). This article will discuss on four important aspects of big data analytics that are relevant for the modern society.

Firstly, it investigates on the examples that have incorporated big data analytics and gives detailed information on the applications that are widely used. These include big data in to banking industry, e-commerce domain and smart city applications including multiple sectors such as education, traffic management and smart grid and guide through the challenges faced by these applications and the requirements of these applications.

Secondly, the discussion focuses on the characteristics of organizations that benefit from Big data technology. A company that can successfully implement big data strategy should be an innovative and data-driven company with great leadership. It should collect everything that it can get, no matter it is online data or offline data. Also, data scientists are essentially required to provide an insight based the analysed data for management to make correct decision.

Thirdly, details on the professional role of CIO which involves bringing such IT capabilities, updating of existing structures and checking feasibility of replacement of existing IT structures with Big data, ethical issues, security issues and governance issues will be presented.

Finally, it will discuss and analyse the components and functions of the strategic plan by taking NASA’s IT strategic plan as an example and demonstrates the role of big data analysis in the strategic plan and a case study of New Zealand’s local company Fonterra on the use of data-driven big data solutions in enterprises.  

Applications of Data Analytics

Data analytics in smart cities

Many governments in the modern era are moving towards adopting the smart city concept into their cities, while some countries have already implemented (Al Nuaimi, Al Neyadi, Mohamed, & Al-jaroodi, 2015). Every city wants to a have a competitive edge over others by having a healthy economy and providing a quality life for the residents to attract skilled people, businessmen and visitors (Chichernea, 2015). In defining the Smart city, it can be described as the incorporation of technology to improve the living standards of the citizens, utilizing the available resources by reducing the waste, cost and improving the efficiency of the council processes (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Futuristic leaders see the opportunities of using big data analytics to derive valuable information and use of cloud technology for data collaboration between government organizations and processes to provide a better service for the residents of the cities (Chichernea, 2015).

There are numerous benefits and newer opportunities being created by integrating big data into smart city applications (Wu, Tsung-chun, Yenchun Jim, & Lytras, 2018). Efficient utilization of resources as in enterprise resource planning applications, help to monitor systems to easily identify waste points and take measures to control and distribute the resources for better management of the cost and resources (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). It offers improved quality of life with the use of more efficient services and improves the transparency and openness with interoperability among different smart city applications (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Big data can be used in many sectors such as education, controlling traffic and electricity management in various smart city applications (Chichernea, 2015).

Smart education applications

In the education sector, it could help in creating smart educational applications that support to build knowledge bases, so that users who are passionate about learning can make use of these applications to gain knowledge (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). One such form is learning analytics which is used to process data about learners that assist in monitoring student and educational institutes (Wang, 2016). It can also be used by educational providers to identify people and trends concerning the education and create customized learning modules (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). These types of applications also open doors for rural residents to acquire the skills and expertise irrespective of their physical location (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). By using these personalized learning model, people tend to be more actively engage in learning compared to standard learning procedures (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). It can be clearly seen by analyzing the two articles by Wang and Al Nuaimi et al. that they both have identified the importance of these applications in terms of measuring performance for the institutes and students.

Smart traffic management applications

When it comes to traffic management, big data applications could be used to identify the traffic patterns and effectively manage the traffic lighting system (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). So that it reduces the traffic congestions which helps to save people’s time that would be wasted on traffic and also contributes to preserving the natural resources by limiting the waste of fuel that would otherwise lose during traffic congestions (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). These can be archived by feeding the traffic sensor data into big data applications where those process the data in real time and provide instructions to lighting systems to manage the traffic (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). It is clearly illustrated the effectiveness of these systems using an example as “implementing smart traffic lights and signals designed by the Traffic21 project in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, the USA obtained significant results, which reduced traffic jams and waiting times resulting in reduced emissions by over 20%” (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015, p. 6).

Another such system that process real-time data using big data with data collected via connected vehicles and alert the drivers to take alternate routes to avoid traffic congestions, which also help to reduce travel time and harmful emissions (Cardenas-Benítez et al., 2016). The key difference between the two articles by Al Nuaimi et al. and Cardenas-Benítez et al. is that the way of capturing data for the processing, respectively one states that the data captured through traffic light sensors and the other states data captured through connected vehicles.  

Smart grid applications

Electricity management and the concept of the smart grid is also gaining popularity which is related to the broad topic of the smart city (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). It uses the information and communication technology to gather data from the electrical grid system and uses big data to analyze those data to identify the usage patterns of the consumers and supplier behaviors (Daki, El Hannani, Aqqal, Haidine, & Dahbi, 2017). The use of two-way communicational devices, smart sensors and smart meters are being used to manage the grid with optimum utilization and to get real-time data for analytical purposes (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). By analyzing the power usage data, the smart grid applications can help the suppliers by suggesting ways to implement dynamic pricing models so that the heavy power usages can be limited with high pricing for peak hours which allows the power grids to operate smoothly (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). On the other hand, it helps the consumers by suggesting them to use electricity cost-effectively by using the massive power consuming devices on non-peak hours (Daki et al., 2017).

Challenges in smart city applications

There are quite many challenges faced when designing and implementing smart city-related applications (Chauhan et al., 2016). One such problem is data that being collected from various data sources comes in different formats, and some are even unstructured (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Before the analysis phase, these data need to clean and classify into structured forms so that the applications become efficient in the decision-making process (Chauhan et al., 2016). Sharing of data and information among the departments and applications is another challenge due to the departmental policies that restrict it (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Data quality issues such as data not being consistent, uncertainty and incompleteness are points to be considered in building smart applications (Chauhan et al., 2016).

Incorporating an adequate level of data security and privacy in building applications is a challenge for the application developers (Daki et al., 2017). Need to follow strict policies to protect the confidential data from reaching the unauthorized people (Chauhan et al., 2016). The technologies used for the big data analytics such as Hadoop and Cassandra lack sufficient level of security (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Another challenge is cost, where the cost of developing the smart city applications are very high due to the complex nature of those applications (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Therefore, extra care should be taken to implement these applications correctly that function according to the expected level of standard (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). The ever-growing population too also a major concern in developing the applications where they should be able to cope up with the rapid growth of data generation (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015).

Requirements of smart city applications

Few requirements need to be taken care of when building smart city applications (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Some are general requirements that are being applied to any application that use big data, while some are more specific requirements for the smart city applications (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Big data management is one such requirement, where it includes the proper management tools to manage the full lifecycle including architecting, developing and implementing the big data application (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). There are specific hardware and technical configurations requirements for the big data frameworks that need to be considerate (Daki et al., 2017). The platforms to process big data needs to meet the performance standards to handle a massive amount of data, stable and reliable enough to process highly intensive data (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). For the data to share with the smart city components there needs to be an intelligent network infrastructure so that the data sharing would happen seamlessly without any interruptions (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). It also needs to have advanced algorithms so that it could efficiently handle the big data meeting the performance criteria (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015).

Security of these applications should be maintaining some acceptable level of standard to protect the sensitive information because many users share some shared platforms (Daki et al., 2017). For some, it would be helpful if these smart city applications can be developed using open standards so that data sharing and communication among the components would be more comfortable (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). The governments should enact some guiding policies to govern the openness and transparency of these applications (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015). Finally, applications should be user-friendly so that the citizens could use them safely and correctly to get the most out of them (Al Nuaimi et al., 2015).  

Data analytics in banking

The banking industry is another popular sector that generates enormous amounts of data every day (Jamar et al., 2017). These comprise of millions of transactions that were made through banks daily which contains vital information and with the help of data analytics that can be used to increase revenues, find new business opportunities, provide quality customer service, identify the marketing trends and to stay ahead of the competitors (Jamar et al., 2017).

Various sectors in the banking domain can use data analytics for their advancements (Jamar et al., 2017). In bank sales management, big data applications are used to send real-time custom-tailored sales messages to customer’s banking applications (Skiera, 2016). Risk management, customer profitability, performance analytics, product innovation, historical analysis and fraud detection are a few main areas that use data analytics (Jamar et al., 2017). The use of data analytics in fraud detection will enhance the banks' security standards which in turn helps to increase the revenue (Jamar et al., 2017). Finally, by using data analytics in banks helps to improve the efficiency of the computer machines and applications by faster processing of the customer queries which reduces the response time (Jamar et al., 2017).

Data analytics in E-business

Big data analytics are being used in the E-business domain quite frequently to reveal the hidden patterns, market trends, identify customer needs and to find useful information for other business needs (Avinash & Babu, 2018). The data obtained from these applications help the companies to develop effective marketing strategies, find new revenue generation sources, improve the customer satisfaction and stay ahead of the competitors (Avinash & Babu, 2018). By the help of these applications, organizations can gain a visualized perspective of the data and help forecast business roadmap (Avinash & Babu, 2018). A research firm that associated with MIT, in 2012, revealed firms that use big data analytics outperformed their competitors by 5% in productivity and 6% in profitability (Avinash & Babu, 2018). Therefore, it is clear that adopting big data adds a lot of value to applications that operate in the e-business sector.

Overall the articles referred under the Big data analytics integration in different applications, all concerned about the privacy of data. However, the level of privacy concern varies among the domain and the sector that these applications used. Moreover, the budgeting for these applications is quite high compared to other software applications due to advanced technology, skilled labor force and infrastructure requirements. Due to these high costs, having a proper plan is essential for these applications. Most of the applications discussed so far had a primary goal of adding convenience to people’s lives otherwise precious time and money of people would be wasted on inconvenient applications

Characteristics of organisations

To become an information-centric enterprise and maintain a leading edge in the fierce market competition, it is obviously not enough just to collect a large amount of data. What are the main characteristics of those companies that have successfully implemented big data strategy?

Data driven

Data-driven is an adjective used to refer to a process or activity that is stimulated by data, rather than merely driven by intuition or personal experience. In other words, the products and the decision are made with solid empirical practices rather than guessing or intuition. It is argued that online firms like Google, Amazon, Facebook and LinkedIn were built based on big data when they start up (Davenport & Dyché, 2013). When developing new products and services, one ambitious thing is that all the implementation is based on collected data. Many of the companies that try this approach are online firms, which have an obvious need to employ data-based products and services (Davenport & Dyché, 2013). One example is Taobao, which uses big data to provide many featured services, such as Goods You May Like, Cities You Want to Go, What You Have Watched Recently and so on. These offerings have promoted millions of new product purchase.

For online service providers, it is easy to collect data, but more and more offline companies start their data collection as well. Rolls-Royce’s engines can collect 100 GB of data during operation, and TomTom can collect about 5.5 billion data sets a day from its global navigation systems. The auto companies have hundreds of sensors on their cars to monitor them and plan how to repair them after they break down. The more data companies collect, the more effective big data strategy will be (Rijmenam., 2018).

To be a real data-driven company is very crucial, and many organizations only pretends to be one. It is common to see executives look for data to support their business decision they already have in their mind (McAfee, Brynjolfsson, Davenport, Patil, & Barton, 2012). Data should always go first and then next should be the decision.  

Information centric culture

Data-driven and information-centric culture look quite similar to each other. But, they have a lot of differences. Data, after all, is useless if people cannot analyse it by correct tools and if there is no right culture in organizations (Rijmenam., 2018). Only when organizations make correct analysis to gain the expected information so that they can make correct business decisions. Those companies successfully implementing big data strategies have built an information-centric culture, and all employees of the company are fully aware of the possibility of good analysis and visual information (Rijmenam., 2018). The better the information is visualized, the better organizations can make better business decisions based on the information (Rijmenam., 2018). US Xpress, the US cargo shipping company, is a good example of this. With the iPad, all the truck drivers in the company can keep all the necessary information in a timely manner, although they are still driving on the road. The entire enterprise takes full advantages of collected data to make decisions.

The information-centric culture is aware of the organization’s information-centric maturity level and striving to constantly improve it. It is aware that being Information-centric is a journey, not a termination because the goal posts are always moving. It is open to change but understands that change requires rigor. That chasing trends without process and requisite rigor could damage the organization.

Strong motivation to innovate and to stay ahead

Companies that implemented big data strategies are normally cutting-edge technology enthusiasts and they do everything to study new technologies to be innovative and stay ahead in the market (Rijmenam., 2018). For example, IBM Research has provided a study annually about what technology trends are for its senior management team for more than 25 years. The analysis looks at the innovations that may impact businesses worldwide, especially the information technology domain. This capability helps drive IBM’s US$6 billion investment in research and development each year.

Big data enables companies to maintain a leading competitive advantage in the fierce market competition and constantly re-discover the enterprise themselves (Rijmenam., 2018). Sears established his own data warehouse since 1980s, while other retail companies were still manually updating customer’s information in spreadsheets at that time. Its technology foresight makes Sears stay ahead of retailers for years.

These companies are leaders of the market and they are both creators of new technologies and early adopters. Their motivation to innovate has enabled them to deploy big data strategies for many years (Rijmenam., 2018).

Centralized data storage

Big data is massive data that can reach millions of GB or more every day (Rijmenam., 2018). To implement big data strategy, organizations should firstly collect adequate data in their hands. One characteristic of big data companies is that they collect everything that they can get: data from social media, data from wearable equipment, data from the workplace, etc (Rijmenam., 2018). GE put sensors on gas turbine engines to collect blades health data which can be generated 500 gigabytes every day (Davenport & Dyché, 2013). Organizations cannot analyse data if they do not have data. It is very important to store data first and decide later if organizations need it (Rijmenam., 2018).

Nowadays, with the rapid development of technologies, the cost of data storage should not be an obstacle. Apache even provides an open-source software named Hadoop for companies around the world to use. With Hadoop, companies can save their original data in unstructured and semi-structured formats to centralized commodity hardware.

Data scientists in the house

As data is easy to obtain for companies, how to analyse it becomes a problem. Analysing terabytes and different types of data is a complicated task, even though many big data start-ups claim that the profits of their products cannot afford an IT department which is quite costly (Rijmenam., 2018). The job of data scientists in companies is to discover patterns and relationships that others have not seen or thought of and turn those findings into actionable information that creates value for the organization (Watson, 2014). It requires technology skillset to do this. Data scientists need to understand different types of big data and how to store them, write code, access data, analyse it, and communicate the results to management in business terms. Data scientists are often curious, like to solve difficult problems, and have advanced degrees in analytics, such as statistics, management science/operational research, computer science, and mathematics (Watson, 2014). That is the reason why it is so expensive to hire a data scientist. All companies deploying big data strategies hire at least one data scientist, while international large companies probably hire more data scientists (Rijmenam., 2018). GE achieved a considerable success in hiring data scientists (Davenport & Dyché, 2013), of which number reaches to 400. Some of them are recruited externally and some of them are transferred from other departments of the company. Apart from this, GE also established a training program attempting to solve the shortage of data scientists.

Great leadership

The leadership in this section specially refers to the ability of handling the relationship between big data and managements. The reason why companies can succeed in the big data ear is not simply because they have sufficient and high-quality data in hands, but also because they have great leadership teams (McAfee et al., 2012).

Executives are normally lack of the knowledge of data mining and data analyzing like data scientists. In the same way, data scientists do not have the ability to tackle business issues. A company with great leadership can build an effective communication for both parties. They can work together and seek to understand each other to create positive outcomes for the organization and its decisions.

Professional role of a CIO

Many organizations in the world are yet to equip themselves with big data capabilities (Krihely, 2018). The CIO’s of such organizations therefore need to update their existing IT structures to include Big data, its storage and analytics structures. Depending on perspectives, executives of organizations handing big data are bringing in such capabilities to their enterprise, while existing organizations who already have such capabilities, are looking at performance and efficiency improvements.

The CIO’s should work along with CEO’s of enterprise to bring in innovation, where the CEO will set the direction and CIO’s contribute by providing an optimal path (Maclever, Okajima, 2014).Professional CIO’s can also contribute to analytics by proper risk management, data design, by establishing security practices, and through management policies. They can also act as a co-ordinating body between data scientists and business leaders, and improve existing businesses or constantly seek opportunities convert analytics into new businesses (Ribeiro, 2014 ).

In a big data environment, CIO’s generally identify key issues where Big Data could make a difference (Krihley, 2018). They will oversee how existing technologies could be integrated. The CIO’s of big data organizations would also look into organizing the right skills to be brought into the organization who can work under the existing as well as future needs of the business. One of the challenges of a professional CIO in such an environment would be to see whether Big data solutions should have communication with the existing platforms they would replace or whether the existing IT capabilities can address such issues.

The CIO’s would certainly check the feasibility of massive efforts where existing technologies are replaced by a Big data environment. In a massive data oriented scenario like Big data, the CIO’s will constantly look into the processes, tools and skills to be brought into the organization. All these authors from different perspectives, point towards the necessity of a CIO having communicative, leadership and technical skills to perform optimally in a big data environment. As evident, the authors put emphasis on leadership, communicative and technical skills of CIO’s for a big data organization. Apart from the above, the professional role of CIO involves ensuring good ethical practices in the organization, security policies in place and complying with good governance practices. The challenges in those fronts are detailed as under.

Ethical issues in Big data

Big data is termed as a disruptive technology where it starts something new or that brings changes to an existing industry (Wessel, 2016). Big data found in a myriad of industries involves various types that include multiple media, audio or video. These new types of data sources increase the size of big data cache. Over the past several years, big data is seen to affect principle of ethics like ownership, decision making, security and privacy (White, Ariyachandra, 2017). Increasingly, organizations use Big data results for decision making that would have influence on ethical aspects.

According to (Saqr, 2011), in a real world scenario, all stages of working data should have transparency applied to it. Stakeholder groups should be informed about how their data is captured and used. But in reality, this may not be the case and data may be used in future and the original stakeholder would not be informed.

Use of Big data may involve exposure of private information. In usual scenario, data should be derecognized to protect it. There is an increasing number of people connected through multiple networks, which facilitates increased data sharing. All these makes protection of privacy a daunting task, especially with information shared through multiple connections and locations all over the world. (White, et al, 2017).

An objective of providing data security is enable data protection from unintended users and unauthorized users. The huge volume of data makes data protection even harder to accomplish. Such lack of data protection itself would become violation of ethics since it reduces the confidence of users on the reliability of data. Another aspect to be considered in Big data environment is data ownership. Data ownership is the sense of having control while real ownership of data rarely exist ( White, et al, 2017 ). Data in big data scenario is sourced from multitude of locations and the recipient organization of data gets a sense of acquisition of data.  

Security Issues in Big data

There is a constant debate from IT industry on whether big data need to be treated separately in terms of network infrastructure or not (Dataquest, 2015). However, for providing security to big data, it poses the same challenges as defining data security strategy as any other technology. Devising the strategy involves identifying data confidentiality levels, classification of the sensitive data, deciding upon location of critical data and secure access models for data and analysis.

According to (Srivastava, Gehlawat, Kaira, 2016) network traffic is important and must be encrypted for smooth functioning. Information technology plays a key role in the world today for Big data, and therefore it necessitates a platform for securing data and management. A well secured big data involves specific security requirements build around the life cycle of big data. This is established by data collection being secured at start followed by data access being secured in the life cycle. Integrity of data however is the key element, based on which a proper assessment of threats in the organization’s big data could be done (Dataquest, 2015).

The collection of data demands higher performance firewalls and security devices on the network, with high end data encryption involved, to handle throughput, application data traffic and connections. Policy creation and its enforcement becomes critical in a big data scenario in comparison to other environments due to the size of data and access count involved with big data environment (Dataquest, 2015).

The amount of data also necessitates the need to prevent data leakage. Technologies that could prevent leakage of information to unauthorised recipients need to be deployed in priority. Use of data integrity systems and intrusion detection systems are recommended in a big data scenario. Organizations also need to provide security irrespective of data location since Big data encompasses information from multiple sources.

With appropriate tools like reporting appliances and dedicated logging, log data could be aggregated from compatible devices and analysed, and reported, apart from archiving network traffic, web contents and security events. This way, it helps an organization in understanding normal activities in a big data environment.

Governance issues in Big data

According to (Shah, 2017), big data governance is having a different approach, since the volume, velocity and variety of data is complicated. Governance of such data then becomes complicated and involves stitching the clusters together where the clusters have different business purposes.

In a big data environment, throughout the data supply cycle, governance of data involves defining and managing those data (Washington, 2018). The author also cites traceability and transparency as significant elements in that could be tracked through metadata and lineage. She claims that a centralized, business oriented governance model with a thorough understanding of data assets is ideal for big data environment.

According to (Tallon, 2013), data governance practices that balances between value creation and risk exposure for better competitive advantage and value maximization through big data could be identified as the key organizational imperative. He also observes that organizational policies that strike an optimal balance between acceptable and affordable risks become key to a good governance regime in big data environment.

Authors (Ballard et al., 2014) identifies the governing principles for Big data governance through a series of tenets. They suggest that a Big data organization should have nimble response to technological changes, internal processes and needs of the customers. Compliance to organizational policies and regulations is another such principle. Such organizational policies involve meeting the stake holder expectations and meeting the privacy laws. Quality Management is another principle where the authors suggest data quality as critical to continued success. Encouraging the outcome from organizational staff, and understanding the business flow becomes the next principle. The next principle comes with understanding the meaning of the data, its classification and protection. Use and reuse of data by encouraging and fostering stewardship is another principle that is critical for big data governance. Management of long term requirements and feedback, fostering innovation and controlling the third party content are the remaining governing principles that are relevant to a big data environment.  

Discuss an existing strategic plan

Strategic plan

In this section, we will introduce strategy plan, using “NASA Information Technology Strategic Plan (Fiscal Years 2018-2021)” as an example, and critically discuss the strategic plan. A strategic plan is a document used for internal communication within an organization, including organization goals, the actions required to achieve them, and all other key elements that arise during the planning process (Rumelt, 2011). The major parts of a strategic plan include mission and vision, core values, SWOT, Objectives, and measurements (Krach, 2016).

Mission and vision

According to Simpson, D. (1994), there were two opposing points of mission and vision. One part believes that a clear vision is essential to promote business ahead, such as Jean-Yves Gueguen of American Express. The other part does not believe that mission and vision are worth much, such as Brian Marsh of Shell. However, many successful companies have unique vision and mission. Mission and vision of the organization can be a part of organization’s culture (Dimitrova & Petrova, 2016). Even some people consider vision and mission useless, mission and vision are important to the senior managers of an organization.

In NASA IT Strategic Plan, The IT mission is to enable the data to be used securely to complete NASA’s Mission. The IT vision is to manage IT as a strategic resource to unleash the power of data (Wynn, 2018).

NASA’s IT mission and vision are very brief and concise. Its IT vision come up with a future state for the sufficient use of NASA’s abundant data, putting data to a key role for the next 4 years. Its IT mission outlines NASA’s purpose and role that will make its vision a reality. NASA’s Mission needs to rely on information technology to achieve, especially data processing technology. From observing the Earth, analysing planets, comets and other objects in the solar system to observing remote objects in deep universe, data that collected tell us more information about the universe (Wynn, 2018). For these reasons, NASA gives clear mission and vision related to data and put data to the key position of its IT strategic plan.  

Core values

Paine (1994) claimed that values statement is a long-lasting core principle that guides organizational strategy and define organizational behaviour. They are important principles that guide leaders and employees in their daily work and long-range decision-making activities. NASA’s IT values aims to be a trusted partner through continuous learning, customer-driven, accountable and responsible, complementing the core values of NASA’s strategic plan and guiding behaviour in achieving NASA’s vision and mission (Wynn, 2018). According to its IT strategic plan, NASA’s IT values have close ties with the core values in strategic plan. The change of strategic plan may directly affect its IT values. However, for NASA, cutting-edge research in IT area is crucial, and no matter how much the plan changes, it should adhere to the long-term core of research in IT area, especially in data innovation security.

SWOT

SWOT analysis is a tool for planning to identify strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats (Helms & Nixon, 2010). Researchers in strategic management (Mintzberg et al., 1998) agree that SWOT is the foundation for solving organizational issues and achieving the desired goals. By listing both unfavourable and favourable external and internal issues in the four quadrants of a SWOT analysis grid, planners can better understand how strengths can be provided to get new opportunities and how weaknesses can decrease progress or magnify organizational threats (Porter, 1991). And according to Hladchenko (2014), SWOT analysis enables organization to focus on specific areas, identify or build its advantage, minimize or eliminate disadvantages, maximize opportunities, and respond to or overcome threats.

In NASA’s IT Strategic Plan, SWOT analysis cannot be found. This may be a major drawback of the plan. If using SWOT analysis, NASA can get the external threats and opportunities as well as the internal weaknesses and strengths. Lambright (2015) stated that several companies, such as SpaceX, brought more competition in the aerospace industry. Competition leaded to the drop of costs and gave NASA a competitive challenge in technology. This can be seen as an external threat. NASA’s advantage is its space technology, experience and space data. Its data strategy is a full use of its advantage. However, SWOT analysis has its weaknesses. It may produce a lot of information, but not all of it is useful (Chermack & Bernadette, 2007). Therefore, some alternative solutions have emerged, such as SVOR (Strengths, Vulnerabilities, Opportunities, and Risks) (Mesly, 2017).

Objectives

Strategic objectives are the starting point of a strategic planning, Objectives indicate the expectation of senior executives (Harrison, 1995). Fonterra: A data-driven organization

Data-driven strategic planning

Strategy planning is a process of defining direction, or strategy, and making decisions on assigning resources to pursue the strategy of an organization (Allison & Kaye, 2011). The first and the most important step in any strategic planning process is to determine the purpose of an organization’s business value. The second step is to determine the current state. Once an organization know the two points, it can begin to seek factors to achieve the goal. In other words, organizations’ business strategy is the guidelines of the other strategic planning (Sinha, 2014).

The fifth key dimension of Big Data

Erl, Khattak, and Buhler (2016) define that big data is an area dedicated to analysing, processing, and storing data from a huge number of different sources. The emergence of big data will bring revolutionary changes in data collection, data processing methods and data scale.

In addition to the four dimensions of volume, velocity, variety, and veracity, value is considered to be the fifth important dimension in Big Data Analysis (BDA). No matter what data, if it is useless to produce value, it is useless and has no contribute to the organization (Chiang, Grover, Liang & Zhang, 2018). The success of BDA requires not only a large amount of infrastructure, tools and knowledge for data analytics and big data processing, but also how BDA can transform data into competitive advantage and strategic value (Mitzner, 2016). This process exists in all aspects of data analysis, including data acquisition, collection, storage, processing, and management. Organizations explore valuable insights through data analysis to create strategic value.

In many cases, the major issue is that organizations are better at data collection rather than analysing data and designing strategies about it (Gerdeman, 2017). Organizations need a business strategy that incorporates big data rather than a big data strategy (Patrizio, 2017). Guided by clear business goals and plan, big data strategy focuses on collecting useful data and extracting useful information that helps executives create business plans. 6.3. A data-driven strategy of Fonterra Here, we use Fonterra as a case of an organization in New Zealand to demonstrate how organization lay out strategies using big data technology and how organization has been influenced by it.

Owned by 10,000 farmers and their families, Fonterra has become a global dairy nutrition company. It employs about 22,000 people all over the world. It has been at the forepart of NZ’s global exports for over 50 years and accounts for 25% of New Zealand’s exports (“Fonterra,” n.d.).

Fonterra’s products are sold in more than 140 countries (“Fonterra,” n.d.). According to Nigel Adler, general manager of global design and a member of Fonterra’s IS leadership team, there are about a billion customs every day. He says that they get and process the data and put it in an experiment by the traditional data approach. They never really know how much of the data is actually valuable to the organization. They should adopt a new architecture and a new operating model to change their approach to data.

Gerben Otter, the company’s global CIO, says that simplifying processes and removing complexity are the two large areas for Fonterra (“CIO100 2018”, 2018). Fonterra adjusts direction and makes clear purpose that data analytics and quality should to be a critical business function.

With the clear business strategy, Adler’s team knew the data that they really needed, and they had a method to extract these data, and a team of experts to find these data. By the application of advanced data analytics and spending three months, his team has identified Fonterra’s significant potential. That is to change the approach to data with a new architecture and a new operating model. The key method to success is elastic compute, elastic storage and open technology.

There are two problems that need to be solved. One is how to assure the quality of the data and what Fonterra could do with it. Another is that they need to reform their analytics strategy (Paredes, 2018).

The cooperation between Fonterra’s Velocity and Innovation team and the Global Information Services team (IS) is very significant for solving the problems. IS is good at delivering the platform and managing the quality of the data. Meanwhile the Velocity and Innovation team gives advanced analytics and drives it throughout the company (Paredes, 2018).

They shifted its analytics architecture strategy from an SAP based architecture to an open cloud-based analytics platform that could quickly transform original data to the data that data scientists could do something with it. As Adler said, Fonterra used the (AWS) data hub to bring data together when needed.

Fonterra’s new data strategy let the data team focus intensely on the industrialized ingestion of data. For example, in one of Fonterra’s most valuable use cases, it collected more than a thousand features from different data source and judged 13 of them were valuable. In the end, only thirteen features were industrialized (Paredes, 2018). Data from the farm provides more effective evidence for Fonterra’s innovation. Analysis of soil moisture data in irrigation trials can provide information to reduce water and fertilizer use. Fonterra establishes an experts group including 8 data scientists to solve problems, monitor exception, and train professionals.

Fonterra’s data-driven strategy promote the construction of data infrastructure and expert teams of the company, creating a data-based analytics and decision-making surrounding. Fonterra is transforming from simply having a large amount of data to create value by deep mining data. This is a long-term process. It is difficult to see whether the strategy is successful or unsuccessful in the short-term. It takes time to test the profound impact of big data on the traditional enterprise.

 

Conclusion

Big data is becoming an important technology trend. This technology has changed the way of obtaining information and business models of an organization. Big data is not a single technology implementation, instead, it is a combination of data and management that evolves over time. Big data makes businesses stronger. The large-scale use of big data analytics technology in smart city, banking, e-commerce and other industries has changed the business model, strategic plan and corporate culture of industries and enterprises. At the same time, organizations are competing in recruiting elite talents of big data. While big data brings huge business opportunities to the industries and enterprises, it also brings challenges in the area of ethics, organizational structure and information management. Data analysis has taken an important role in NASA’s IT Strategic plan and the data-driven strategic planning of Fonterra in New Zealand. Big data analytics should be directly integrated into an organization’s strategic plan and not just at the technical level. Big data technology is becoming more and more complex, and what we need is to accept this change and use this new technology ethically and legally to change our world.
